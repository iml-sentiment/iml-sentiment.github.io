Instructions

Please carefully read the following instructions, using the arrows and on the left/right to change pages. You will be able to start the survey when you reach the sixth page.

In this survey, you will help us understand how people perceive the sentiment (positive or negative) of movie reviews. There are {{totalIter}} questions; each shows one word and asks you to judge whether it is relevant for determining the review’s sentiment.

You will receive 1.45 dollars for your effort if and only if you finish all the {{totalIter}} questions.

Expect to spend around 10-15 minutes to complete this survey. Please do not accept the survey if you cannot commit to this time duration.

Take the question about "impressive"; you will need to determine if the word is "Relevant" for predicting sentiment or if it isn’t helpful for deciding that the sentiment is positive or negative ("Irrelevant"). For context, you are shown example review fragments containing the word (right side of the screen), but we want to understand the general case, for example, so we could use the words to train a machine to predict the sentiment of other reviews not yet written.

As you tackle the question, the computer will try to help by providing an estimated score (between 0-100), that shows how relevant or irrelevant it thinks the word is. This score might help you with your judgment (the higher the score, the better the computer thinks it can make the call), but be aware that the score may sometimes be wrong.

We display the estimated scores along with the options you can take (Relevant/Irrelevant), with the higher scored one highlighted with an orange background Relevant, 80, and the other one with blue Irrelevant, 43.

You should submit your decision with on one of the two submit buttons (labeled relevant / irrelevant); this will take you to the next question.

You can only submit after the question has been displayed for {{ enableSubmitSecond }} seconds.
